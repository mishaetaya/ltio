Manifesto

Mission

The objective of this site is two-fold:

  • Give voice to credible ideas bourne by operators, builders and those who roll up their sleeves
  • Tell interesting stories backed by interesting analyses or real life case studies

Too often nowadays what we find on the internet are long-winded rants without any hard data or interesting analysis. Or, even worse (and far more often), pieces written by people who don't have any first hand experience about the subject matter they write about.

So, we decided to create a space, interesting stories can be told by the people that lived them supported by novel analyses or data noone has thought to include. We live in a world with boundless information - we want to put it to good use.

Types of Stories

We will focus on writing three types of stories:

  • 'here's how' - stories, or strategies, for how to change something for the better. But these are not whimsical ideas, rather strategies with supporting data and scenarios for impact.

  • 'left unsaid' - situational or case study oriented stories about challenges, conflicts or controversies that need to be resolved in order for success to occur

  • 'by the numbers' - data based assessment of interesting topics, happenings or phenomena

Guiding principles

  • Our contributors must have walked the walk - our contributors are all people that have had first hand experience with whatever they are writing about. That means - if we're writing about investing it is by someone who has invested his/her own and other people's money, if it's a startup article it is by someone who has been a part of one in the past. These are not hard and fast rules - a good days labor can come in many ways, but our content comes from people that have lived it.

  • We admittedly can not cover every angle, and we'll be honest about this - every article, or story, will conclude with a list of things/topics/angles we didn’t consider, so that you can understand the limitations, and if you're interested you can know how to explore further on your own.

  • Each article will come with data or a case study - to the extent possible we will support our facts either through case study or hard numbers, so as to avoid generalizations and vaguery.

  • Each article will provide some action you can try - these sections will be known as "so what’s the point?"" - every article must have a tactical piece of advice or an anecdote that the reader can experiment with (doesn’t matter if everyone agrees with it).
